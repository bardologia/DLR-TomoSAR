\subsection{Context budget and patch size}
\label{sec:method_patch}

How much spatial context should the network see? The supervision fixes the answer. The label at pixel $x$ is a deterministic functional --- spectral scan and mixture fit --- of the sample covariance
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathbf{R}}(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{w^2} \sum_{x' \in B(x)} \mathbf{y}(x')\,\mathbf{y}(x')^{\mathsf{H}},
  \label{eq:label_cov}
\end{equation}
estimated over the $w \times w$ window $B(x)$ centred on $x$, where $\mathbf{y}(x')$ is the full-stack sample vector. Two consequences follow, independent of scene content. First, the label at $x$ depends on the data only through $B(x)$. Second, two labels share input samples exactly when their windows overlap: at offset $\tau = (\tau_a, \tau_r)$ the shared count is $(w - |\tau_a|)_+ (w - |\tau_r|)_+$, with $(\cdot)_+ = \max(\cdot, 0)$. Under speckle that decorrelates at the resolution-cell scale, the label autocorrelation is therefore triangular per axis, $\rho(\tau) = (1 - |\tau|/w)_+$, and exactly zero beyond $w$: disjoint windows average disjoint, independent samples.

The label is a noisy estimate of the underlying spectrum from a finite look budget, and the reducible part of the held-out error is the distance to that underlying statistic, not to the label realisation. A larger patch lowers it only by supplying additional looks that are certifiably tied to the same statistic --- looks from pixels whose windows overlap $B(x)$, all of which lie within distance $w$ of $x$. The label construction therefore fixes a band for the useful patch. Below $W = w$ the patch withholds inputs that generated its own target: the minimum useful patch is the label window itself. Above it, a patch of side $W$ captures $\min(W, 2w)^2$ of the informative square, growing quadratically until $W = 2w$ and exactly flat beyond: held-out accuracy saturates at twice the window, independently of the input stack size $n$.

The saturation point is the law's sharpest prediction: because $\min(W, 2w)^2$ contains no property of the input stack, the knee must sit at the window scale for every stack size. The patch sweep of Section~\ref{sec:experiments_ablations} confirms it: across stacks of $2$--$15$ passes, held-out accuracy saturates at $40$--$56$\,px against the predicted $2w = 40$\,px, flat in $n$ (Figure~\ref{fig:patch_sweep}). Appendix~\ref{app:patch_law} gives the full derivation and the complete sweep data.
