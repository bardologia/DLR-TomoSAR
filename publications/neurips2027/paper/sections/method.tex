\subsection{Context budget and patch size}
\label{sec:method_patch}

How much spatial context should the network see? The supervision fixes the answer. The label at pixel $x$ is the deterministic spectral scan and mixture fit $\phi$ applied to the sample covariance of the $w \times w$ window $B(x)$ centred on $x$ ($w = 20$\,px in production):
\begin{equation}
  \ell(x) \;=\; \phi\bigl(\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)\bigr),
  \qquad
  \hat{\mathbf{R}}(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{w^2} \sum_{x' \in B(x)} \mathbf{y}(x')\,\mathbf{y}(x')^{\mathsf{H}},
  \label{eq:label_cov}
\end{equation}
with $\mathbf{y}$ the full-stack sample vector. Two assumptions, both already made by the labelling pipeline, carry the analysis: speckle decorrelates at the resolution-cell scale, so distinct pixels are independent; and the covariance is locally constant at the window scale, $w$ being the homogeneity scale of the scene.

Write $x' = x + \tau$ for the pixel at offset $\tau = (\tau_a, \tau_r)$. Two consequences are exact. Each label is a function of its own window alone, so disjoint windows yield independent labels:
\begin{equation}
  \lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \ge w
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  B(x) \cap B(x') = \emptyset
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \ell(x) \indep \ell(x').
  \label{eq:independence}
\end{equation}
Overlapping windows split over their intersection into a shared and a private sum:
\begin{align}
  w^2\,\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)  &= \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \;+ \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \setminus B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}},
  \label{eq:shared_private}\\
  w^2\,\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x') &= \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \;+ \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x') \setminus B(x)} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}}.
\end{align}
The shared sum collects
\begin{equation}
  \bigl|B(x) \cap B(x')\bigr| \;=\; (w - |\tau_a|)_+\,(w - |\tau_r|)_+
  \label{eq:shared_count}
\end{equation}
looks, with $(\cdot)_+$ the positive part. The private sums are independent of each other and of the shared one, so every link between two labels flows through the shared looks, and the correlation between corresponding entries of the two estimates is the shared fraction of the window,
\begin{equation}
  \rho(\tau) \;=\; \Bigl(1 - \tfrac{|\tau_a|}{w}\Bigr)_{\!+}\Bigl(1 - \tfrac{|\tau_r|}{w}\Bigr)_{\!+},
  \label{eq:triangle}
\end{equation}
the self-convolution of the boxcar, inherited by the labels through the linearisation of $\phi$. The link drains linearly and dies exactly at the window scale,
\begin{equation}
  \lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \to w:
  \qquad
  \bigl|B(x) \cap B(x')\bigr| \to 0,
  \qquad
  \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \to 0,
  \qquad
  \rho(\tau) \to 0,
  \label{eq:drain}
\end{equation}
meeting the independence of Eq.~(\ref{eq:independence}) at the boundary. Figure~\ref{fig:corr_law} traces the mechanism: the overlap is the correlation.

The law follows from the held-out risk. The label is a noisy realisation of the underlying statistic,
\begin{equation}
  \ell(x) \;=\; \phi\bigl(\mathbf{R}(x)\bigr) + \varepsilon(x),
  \label{eq:label_noise}
\end{equation}
with $\varepsilon$ the estimation noise of the finite $w^2$-look budget, and the test loss of a predictor $f$ splits accordingly:
\begin{equation}
  \E \norm{f - \ell}^2 \;=\; \E \norm{f - \phi(\mathbf{R})}^2 \;+\; \E \norm{\varepsilon}^2.
  \label{eq:risk_decomp}
\end{equation}
Only the first term is reducible, and it measures the distance to the statistic, not to the label realisation. The window geometry then sorts the pixels around $x$ into three regimes. Within $w/2$, the pixel stacks are looks of Eq.~(\ref{eq:label_cov}): they formed the label. Within $w$, the windows overlap and the labels share information through the shared looks, a common component in their covariance estimates (Eq.~\ref{eq:shared_private}). Beyond $w$, every sample is independent of the label, and any contribution rests on a homogeneity prior the label construction neither uses nor certifies. Table~\ref{tab:context_regimes} gives the overview.

\begin{table}[H]
  \centering
  \caption{The three offset regimes around pixel $x$: pixel stacks inside the window form the label, overlapping windows share looks and correlate, disjoint windows are independent.}
  \label{tab:context_regimes}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
    \toprule
    offset from $x$ & geometry & link to $\ell(x)$ & certified role in the patch \\
    \midrule
    $\lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \le w/2$      & pixel inside $B(x)$ & look of Eq.~(\ref{eq:label_cov})  & forms the label \\
    $w/2 < \lVert\tau\rVert_\infty < w$    & windows overlap     & shared looks, $\rho(\tau) > 0$    & averages away $\varepsilon$ \\
    $\lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \ge w$        & windows disjoint    & independent, $\rho(\tau) = 0$     & none \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The certified context is therefore the square of side $2w$ centred on the pixel, of which a patch of side $W$ captures
\begin{equation}
  L(W) \;=\; \min(W, 2w)^2
  \label{eq:ceiling}
\end{equation}
looks: quadratic growth up to the ceiling, exactly flat beyond. The floor is immediate, since a patch narrower than the window withholds inputs that generated its own target. The useful patch is the band
\begin{equation}
  w \;\le\; W \;\le\; 2w,
  \label{eq:band}
\end{equation}
whose upper end carries the law's sharpest prediction: Eq.~(\ref{eq:ceiling}) contains no property of the input stack, so held-out accuracy must saturate at the window scale for every stack size. The sweep of Section~\ref{sec:experiments_ablations} confirms it (Table~\ref{tab:patch_sweep}, Figure~\ref{fig:patch_sweep}).

Three remarks bound the scope. Organised structure larger than a resolution cell correlates samples across disjoint windows and can only soften the ceiling upward, never create an optimum below it. The law bounds \emph{certified} information: a model may exploit scene regularities beyond the ceiling, they are simply not guaranteed by the label construction. And its only constant is the pipeline's own $w$, so the law transfers to a new scene by re-reading the window from that scene's configuration; whether the observed saturation transfers is untested (Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).
