\subsection{Context budget and patch size}
\label{sec:method_patch}

How much spatial context should the network see? The supervision fixes the answer. The label at pixel $x$ is $\ell(x) = \phi\bigl(\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)\bigr)$, where $\phi$ denotes the deterministic spectral scan and mixture fit and
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathbf{R}}(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{w^2} \sum_{x' \in B(x)} \mathbf{y}(x')\,\mathbf{y}(x')^{\mathsf{H}}
  \label{eq:label_cov}
\end{equation}
is the sample covariance over the $w \times w$ window $B(x)$ centred on $x$ ($w = 20$\,px in production), with $\mathbf{y}(x')$ the full-stack sample vector. The analysis rests on two assumptions the labelling pipeline already makes. Speckle decorrelates at the resolution-cell scale, so sample vectors at distinct pixels are independent. And the covariance is approximately constant at the window scale: the boxcar average of Eq.~(\ref{eq:label_cov}) is consistent only where $\mathbf{R}$ is locally constant, and $w$ is chosen as the homogeneity scale of the scene.

Write $x' = x + \tau$ for an offset $\tau = (\tau_a, \tau_r)$, with $\lVert\tau\rVert_\infty = \max(|\tau_a|, |\tau_r|)$. Two consequences are exact. The label depends on its own window alone --- $\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)$, and with it $\ell(x)$, is a function of $\{\mathbf{y}(p)\}_{p \in B(x)}$ and of nothing else --- and disjoint windows yield independent labels:
\begin{equation}
  \lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \ge w
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  B(x) \cap B(x') = \emptyset
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \ell(x) \indep \ell(x').
  \label{eq:independence}
\end{equation}
For $\lVert\tau\rVert_\infty < w$ each windowed sum splits over the window intersection into a shared part and a private part:
\begin{align}
  w^2\,\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)  &= \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \;+ \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \setminus B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}},
  \label{eq:shared_private}\\
  w^2\,\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x') &= \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \;+ \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x') \setminus B(x)} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}}.
\end{align}
The first sum is common to both estimates and collects
\begin{equation}
  \bigl|B(x) \cap B(x')\bigr| \;=\; (w - |\tau_a|)_+\,(w - |\tau_r|)_+
  \label{eq:shared_count}
\end{equation}
looks, with $(\cdot)_+ = \max(\cdot, 0)$; the second sums draw on disjoint pixel sets, private to each window. The private sums are independent of each other and of the shared sum, so every statistical link between two labels flows through the looks their windows share, and the correlation between corresponding entries of $\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x)$ and $\hat{\mathbf{R}}(x')$ is the shared fraction of the window,
\begin{equation}
  \rho(\tau) \;=\; \Bigl(1 - \tfrac{|\tau_a|}{w}\Bigr)_{\!+}\Bigl(1 - \tfrac{|\tau_r|}{w}\Bigr)_{\!+},
  \label{eq:triangle}
\end{equation}
the self-convolution of the boxcar --- triangular per axis, inherited by the labels to first order in the linearisation of $\phi$. The link drains linearly with the offset and dies exactly at the window scale,
\begin{equation}
  \lVert\tau\rVert_\infty \to w:
  \qquad
  \bigl|B(x) \cap B(x')\bigr| \to 0,
  \qquad
  \sum_{p \,\in\, B(x) \cap B(x')} \mathbf{y}_p\,\mathbf{y}_p^{\mathsf{H}} \to 0,
  \qquad
  \rho(\tau) \to 0,
  \label{eq:drain}
\end{equation}
meeting the independence of Eq.~(\ref{eq:independence}) at the boundary. Figure~\ref{fig:corr_law} traces the mechanism: the overlap is the correlation.

The law follows from the held-out risk. Writing $\ell(x) = \phi(\mathbf{R}(x)) + \varepsilon(x)$, with $\varepsilon$ the estimation noise of the finite $w^2$-look budget, the test loss of a predictor $f$ decomposes as $\E \norm{f - \ell}^2 = \E \norm{f - \phi(\mathbf{R})}^2 + \E \norm{\varepsilon}^2$: the reducible part is the distance to the underlying statistic, not to the label realisation. A larger patch lowers it only by supplying additional looks that are informative about $\mathbf{R}(x)$, and the label construction certifies which looks those are. Pixels within $w$ of $x$ are anchored to the label through shared samples (Eq.~\ref{eq:shared_private}): their looks sample the same $\mathbf{R}(x)$, averaging away $\varepsilon$. Pixels farther than $w$ are exactly independent of every sample that formed $\ell(x)$; any contribution from them rests on extrapolating homogeneity across windows that share no data --- a scene prior the label construction neither uses nor certifies.

The certified context is therefore the square of side $2w$ centred on $x$, and a patch of side $W$ captures
\begin{equation}
  L(W) \;=\; \min(W, 2w)^2
  \label{eq:ceiling}
\end{equation}
of it: the pooled look count grows quadratically until $W = 2w$ and is exactly flat beyond. The floor is immediate --- a patch narrower than the window withholds inputs that generated its own target, so the minimum useful patch is $W = w$, at which point the patch holds exactly the label's own $w^2$ looks. The useful patch is the band $w \le W \le 2w$, and the upper end carries the law's sharpest prediction: since Eq.~(\ref{eq:ceiling}) contains no property of the input stack, held-out accuracy must saturate at the window scale for every stack size $n$. The sweep of Section~\ref{sec:experiments_ablations} confirms it (Table~\ref{tab:patch_sweep}, Figure~\ref{fig:patch_sweep}).

Three remarks bound the law's scope. Organised structure larger than a resolution cell --- building faces, slopes --- correlates samples across disjoint windows and can only soften the ceiling upward: it delays saturation, it cannot create an optimum below $2w$. The law bounds \emph{certified} information, not what priors can add: a model may exploit scene regularities beyond $2w$, they are simply not guaranteed by the label construction. And its only constant is the pipeline's own $w$, so it transfers to a new scene by re-reading the window from that scene's configuration --- whether the observed saturation transfers is untested (Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).
